GME Toolkit

We hope that these resources will help you engage your residents and fellows in this important civic activity. Our goals are to help our residents:

  • keep their voting registration up-to-date
  • take advantage of mail-in voting when available, and
  • become informed voters

Subject: National Voter Registration Day

Hello Everyone,

Happy #NationalVoterRegistrationDay! We think it is important for our students, residents, and fellows to be involved in state and national elections. Please remember to vote in the election on .

Residents and fellows sometimes have schedule conflicts that preclude voting in state and national elections. Every state allows you to vote by mail - it's a convenient, secure way to vote while also protecting your health. If you have yet to do so, please take a minute to check your voter registration and apply for absentee voting. Visit https://vote.health/HENRYFORD for a quick walkthrough on how to register to vote and obtain an absentee ballot.

Here are some unbiased sources for research on the issues and candidates. If you know of other good, non-partisan sources, please share with us and your friends.

  • National, state candidates: https://votesmart.org/, https://ballotpedia.org/, https://www.vote411.org/
  • Local races: https://www.vote411.org/

If there is anything that medical education can do to facilitate this, please let us know! We welcome your suggestions on other ways we can facilitate your involvement in the political process. Please reach out if you have any questions or feedback.

Your vote matters!
